Sustainability
Matters

What is
sustainability?

Sustainability is broadly defined as meeting the needs of the present generation without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs. Sustainable programs are those that result from an institution's commitment to environmental, social and economic health, or the ”triple bottom line.” Sustainability has both individual and institutional applicability, and is usually a balancing act.

Sustainability creates and maintains conditions under which humans and nature can co-exist in productive harmony. Everything we need in order to survive and thrived relies either directly or indirectly on our natural environment.

And how does it relate to design?

Sustainability in design is the philosophy of designing products or services to comply with the principles of social, economic, and ecological sustainability.

Through general guidelines and careful material usage, dietiker remains cautious.

general guidelines

01

If possible, we utilise packaging materials such as cardboard and air cushion foils.

02

We dispose of waste from treated wood in the refuse incinerator and untreated wood is utilised for the inhouse wood heating system.

03

Our suppliers are urged to actively support us with environmental management.

Through careful use of solid wood and board material, laquers and stains, upholstery, coverings, metals, and plastics, dietiker aims to create ecological balance.

how to get involved

01

02

Be a concious consumer and support companies who are practicing sustainablity in their practices.

03
